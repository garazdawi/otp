[1m  trunc(Number)[0m

  Truncates the decimals of [4mNumber[0m.

  See also [4mround/1[0m, [4mfloor/1[0m, and [4mceil/1[0m.

[1mExamples[0m

    1> trunc(5.7).
    5
    2> trunc(-5.7).
    -5
    3> trunc(5).
    5
    4> trunc(36028797018963969.0).
    36028797018963968

  In the last example, [4mtrunc(36028797018963969.0)[0m evaluates to [4m[0m
  [4m36028797018963968[0m. This happens because the number [4m[0m
  [4m36028797018963969.0[0m cannot be represented exactly as a
  floating-point value. Instead, it is represented as [4m[0m
  [4m36028797018963968.0[0m, which is the closest representable
  floating-point value. See Representation of Floating Point Numbers
  for additional information.
